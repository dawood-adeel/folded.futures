Our mission

We bring children from displaced families together to imagine and build realistic dream rooms — a gentle, creative way to restore hope, surface stories, and motivate future goals.

  • Participants: 20–40 students
  • Materials: Cardboard, clay, paints, paper, markers, glue, scissors
  • Time spent: 2 sessions (building + decoration) — ~3–4 hours total

Project overview

This project gathered children who had experienced displacement and asked them to design their ideal, realistic bedroom inside a cardboard model. We documented drawings, conducted short interviews about emotions and aspirations, took photos and videos of the process, and gathered testimonials about how creating helped them.

Materials supplied

  • Cardboard boxes (one per group)
  • Modeling clay
  • Glazed / colored paper
  • Paint, markers, colored A4 sheets
  • Glue, scissors, tape

Session flow

  1. Welcome & pep talk (motivation + safety)
  2. Model construction (structure & furniture)
  3. Decoration & storytelling
  4. Interviews & documentation

Testimonials & Interviews

Short quotes and interview summaries from participants and teachers.

Ahmed (age 11)

"When I painted the carpet pink it felt like my aunt’s blanket at home — warm and safe. Building the little bed with my hands made me realise I can make a real place for someone someday. I want to be an engineer so I can build homes that keep families together."

Fatima (age 12)

"Designing my room made me feel safe again. I drew a window with plants because my mother used to keep one at home — it smelled like hope. I want to be a teacher so I can help children smile like I did today."

Teacher — Ms. Noor

"This activity opened doors. Students who rarely spoke began telling small memories — a smell, a colour, a wish. I saw them become proud of their choices and speak about their futures."
